# Medical AI Safety, Clinical Ethics & Compliance Charter (MEDICAL_AI_SAFETY)

The integration of artificial intelligence into health diagnostic workflows demands the highest standard of clinical responsibility, safety design, and user protection. The **Health Lab Report Analyzer & Tracker** is built around strict medical AI safety principles to serve as a supportive clinical translator rather than a diagnostician.

---

## 1. Core Clinical Design Philosophy: "Assistant, Not Doctor"
AI systems should never make independent therapeutic decisions. The core intent of our multimodal LLM parsing pipeline is **cognitive offloading and translation**:
- **Democratization of Health Records**: Complex, jargon-heavy laboratory reports (with markers like *Apolipoprotein B-100*, *TSH*, or *BUN*) are translated into plain, compassionate, and easy-to-understand language.
- **Supportive Context**: Every extracted biomarker explains what the marker represents and why it is clinically monitored, empowering patients to have more productive, informed conversations with their primary physicians.
- **Strict Therapeutic Boundaries**: The AI pipeline is strictly forbidden from proposing prescription dosages, establishing definitive disease diagnoses, or recommending high-risk medical procedures. Recommendations are strictly bound to supportive lifestyle strategies (e.g., "increase dietary iron via spinach," "engage in aerobic cardiovascular exercise," or "consult an endocrinologist").

---

## 2. Mandatory Human-In-The-Loop & Medical Disclaimer Infrastructure
To prevent over-reliance on AI outputs, the application integrates prominent, persistent safety disclaimers:
- **Primary Clinical Guardrail**: Every parsed PDF/image analysis result displays a mandatory, high-contrast disclaimer:
  > **⚠️ IMPORTANT SAFETY WARNING**: *This analysis is generated by an artificial intelligence assistant for educational and supportive purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified physician or primary healthcare provider before making any dietary, medical, or lifestyle changes.*
- **No Self-Treatment Guidance**: Any abnormal biomarker flag (e.g., low Hemoglobin or high Fasting Glucose) is accompanied by a direct call-to-action to consult a qualified medical provider to review the findings in a clinical context.

---

## 3. Strict Reference Range Guardrails
- **Objective Clinical Standards**: High, low, and normal flags are determined mathematically by comparing the extracted numeric biomarker value directly against the verbatim reference range displayed on the primary laboratory report:
  - If value < lower reference bound $\rightarrow$ flagged as `low`.
  - If value > upper reference bound $\rightarrow$ flagged as `high`.
  - Else $\rightarrow$ flagged as `normal`.
  This prevents the AI from inventing subjective or arbitrary reference standards, maintaining strict objective alignment with the laboratory's specific diagnostic instrumentation.

---

## 4. Privacy, HIPAA Alignment & Patient Data Isolation
- **Strict Account Boundaries**: Patient data is isolated using session authentication. Lab reports are assigned to unique `userId` keys inside `database.json`. No patient can query, view, or search medical records or trend data belonging to another user.
- **Audit Trails**: Time-stamped logs of report uploads are recorded to ensure that a patient can track their historical data trends chronologically, minimizing the risk of diagnostic tracking errors.
- **Local Persistency**: No medical records are sold, distributed, or processed by unauthorized third-party trackers, respecting standard HIPAA guidelines regarding protected health information (PHI).
